Chapter 1 — Systems of Equations & Matrices

The computational foundation: from systems of linear equations to matrix algebra and determinants. Every topic includes theory, worked examples, and practice problems.

S

1.1 — Systems of Linear Equations

Gaussian elimination, row echelon form, RREF, free variables, and parametric solutions.

\(Ax = b\)
M

1.2 — Matrix Algebra

Matrix operations, properties, transpose, symmetric matrices, and matrix powers.

\((AB)^T = B^T A^T\)
I

1.3 — Matrix Inverses

Elementary matrices, invertibility criteria, computing inverses, and applications.

\(A^{-1}A = I\)
D

1.4 — Determinants

Cofactor expansion, properties, Cramer's rule, and geometric interpretation.

\(\det(AB) = \det(A)\det(B)\)

Chapter 2 — Vector Spaces

The abstract framework: understanding the structure of solution sets through subspaces, bases, dimension, and the rank theorem.

V

2.1 — Vector Spaces & Subspaces

Definition, examples, closure properties, column space, null space of a matrix.

\(\text{Col}(A),\; \text{Nul}(A)\)
L

2.2 — Span & Linear Independence

Linearly independent sets, spanning sets, and the connection to systems of equations.

\(c_1\mathbf{v}_1 + \cdots + c_n\mathbf{v}_n = \mathbf{0}\)
B

2.3 — Basis & Dimension

Basis for a subspace, dimension, rank theorem, and coordinate systems.

\(\dim(\text{Col}\,A) + \dim(\text{Nul}\,A) = n\)
R

2.4 — Rank & the Fundamental Theorem

Row space, column space, null space, left null space, and their dimensions.

\(\text{rank}(A) = \text{rank}(A^T)\)
C

2.5 — Change of Basis

Coordinate vectors, change-of-basis matrices, and applications to computer graphics.

\([\mathbf{x}]_{\mathcal{C}} = P_{\mathcal{C} \leftarrow \mathcal{B}} [\mathbf{x}]_{\mathcal{B}}\)

Chapter 3 — Eigenvalues & Diagonalization

The spectral perspective: finding eigenvalues and eigenvectors, diagonalizing matrices, and applying these to dynamical systems and stochastic models.

E

3.1 — Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors

Characteristic equation, finding eigenvalues and eigenvectors, eigenspaces.

\(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\)
D

3.2 — Diagonalization

Diagonalizability criteria, computing \(P\) and \(D\) such that \(A = PDP^{-1}\).

\(A^k = PD^kP^{-1}\)
M

3.3 — Markov Chains

Stochastic matrices, steady-state vectors, and long-term behavior of systems.

\(\mathbf{x}_{k+1} = P\mathbf{x}_k\)
S

3.4 — Dynamical Systems

Discrete dynamical systems, trajectories, attractors, and stability analysis.

\(\mathbf{x}_k = c_1 \lambda_1^k \mathbf{v}_1 + c_2 \lambda_2^k \mathbf{v}_2\)
C

3.5 — Complex Eigenvalues

Complex roots of the characteristic equation, rotation-dilation interpretation.

\(\lambda = a \pm bi\)

Chapter 4 — Orthogonality & Least Squares

Geometry in \(\mathbb{R}^n\): inner products, orthogonal projections, the Gram-Schmidt process, and least-squares solutions for data fitting.

I

4.1 — Inner Products & Norms

Dot product, length, distance, angle between vectors, Cauchy-Schwarz inequality.

\(\|\mathbf{u}\| = \sqrt{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u}}\)
O

4.2 — Orthogonal & Orthonormal Sets

Orthogonal bases, orthogonal matrices, and their computational advantages.

\(Q^TQ = I\)
G

4.3 — Gram-Schmidt & QR

Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process and QR factorization.

\(A = QR\)
L

4.4 — Least-Squares Problems

Normal equations, projections, data fitting, and linear regression.

\(A^TA\hat{\mathbf{x}} = A^T\mathbf{b}\)

Chapter 5 — SVD & Applications

The capstone: Singular Value Decomposition, principal component analysis, linear transformations, and real-world applications from image compression to Google's PageRank.

S

5.1 — Singular Value Decomposition

Computing the SVD, geometric interpretation, and low-rank approximations.

\(A = U\Sigma V^T\)
P

5.2 — PCA & Dimensionality Reduction

Principal Component Analysis, covariance matrices, and data visualization.

\(C = \frac{1}{n-1}X^TX\)
T

5.3 — Linear Transformations

Geometric transformations in 2D/3D, rotation, reflection, shear, and homogeneous coordinates.

\(T(\mathbf{x}) = A\mathbf{x}\)
A

5.4 — PageRank & Network Analysis

Google's PageRank algorithm, adjacency matrices, graph Laplacians, and network science.

\(\mathbf{r} = M\mathbf{r}\)
